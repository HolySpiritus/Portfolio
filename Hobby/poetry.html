Snipers poetry

Path of Hope (newest)

I wanted to escape forever,
But these loud noises pull me back, however.
I see countless people every day,
And feel like I'm always in their way.

I came to a forest seeking peace,
But found much more than mere release.
The sky was clearer than before,
And mountains stretched forevermore.

Happy animals crossed my path,
Following them, I forgot my past.
A sense of endless calm arose,
And in my heart, belief reposed.

I watched the birds twirl as they sang,
Their melody left me in awe, still rang.
I remembered love I used to hold,
Yet never cherished, never told.

"I forgive myself," I softly swore,
"I want to find hope once more."

Breathe.

I wake up and I breathe,
For there's another journey indeed.
I grab my stuff and always leave,
For my life is filled with grief.

I stand up and I breathe,
Though my hands now bleed.
I wish to end this grief,
And find eternal peace.

I walk and I breathe,
Faces sadder than they seem.
They wish to shed a tear,
But feel there's no need.

I looked at her, couldn't breathe,
She held my wounds, stopping the bleed.
She took my tears in need,
Showing my life was worth indeed.

She took my hand and guided me,
To a night forest, a sight unseen.
Beauty strung in every tree,
I finally felt a peaceful weave.

I was proud to breathe,
For I had ended my grief.
